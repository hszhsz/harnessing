# Appendix C: Notes & Sources

## About This Appendix

This appendix is the unified reference index for *Harnessing: A History of Human Productivity*. Every numbered footnote in the main text (e.g. `[^1]`) resolves here, grouped chapter by chapter. Readers can use it two ways: as a fact-checking companion while reading the body text, and as a research entry point that links the book's claims to the broader scholarly conversations they emerged from.

The book covers a 2.6-million-year arc from Oldowan stone tools to AI agents, drawing on paleoanthropology, energy history, economic history, and computer science. To keep the body text readable, the book uses a "light footnotes, consolidated sources" convention: footnotes appear only where a specific number, a contested judgment, or a non-obvious claim warrants documentation, with full bibliographic detail deferred to this appendix. Citations follow standard academic form — monographs as "Author, *Title* (Publisher, year)"; articles as "Author, 'Title,' *Journal* vol., no. (year): pages"; arXiv preprints and institutional reports retain their URLs for direct retrieval.

As of this release, the chapters with complete footnotes are: Chapter 1 (Hand), Chapter 3 (Reins), Chapter 10 (Electrification), Chapter 14 (LLMs), and Chapter 16 (Agent Economics). Footnotes for the remaining chapters (Introduction, Chapters 2, 4–9, 11–13, 15, 17, and Appendices A and B) will be added in subsequent releases and are marked here as placeholders. Community pull requests are welcome.

Readers looking for an opinionated next-step reading list — rather than a citation index — should turn to **Appendix C-2: Further Reading**.
