# Part V — The Age of Agents

*(2020s – )*

---

> *"Every previous harness connected a human hand to a force in the world. This one connects a human intention to a mind."*

Every previous age in this book has a comfortable feature: it is over. We can look back at the steam engine, the printing press, the waterwheel, the first sharpened stone, with the calm of people who already know how the story turned out. The Age of Agents does not offer that comfort. We are inside it. The early chapters of its history are being written, quite literally, in the same months in which this book is being read.

What is being harnessed in this Age is, for the first time, *intelligence itself*. Not symbolic computation — that was the previous Age — but the messier, more general capacity to interpret an unclear situation, weigh competing considerations, draw on a vast hinterland of prior knowledge, and produce a response that is sensible in context. The large language model, trained on much of what humanity has written down, turned out to be the first artifact in history that could do this reliably enough to act on. And the moment such an artifact existed, a question that had been latent for decades suddenly became urgent: what if you gave it *tools*?

That question is the seed of this entire Age. A model on its own can answer; a model with tools can *do*. A model with tools and a loop — perceive, reason, act, observe — can keep doing, for hours, while the human who issued the instruction goes to sleep. The harness that connects model to tools is, this time, made not of leather but of code: a software architecture for routing intention into action and feeding the consequences back. The community that builds these systems, with quiet historical resonance, has come to call this architecture, simply, *the harness*.

Three chapters explore the unfolding. **Chapter 14 — Large Language Models** examines the strange new substance at the heart of it all: a statistical engine that nevertheless seems to traffic in meaning, and the long history of natural-language interfaces that quietly prepared the ground for it. **Chapter 15 — AI Agents** opens up the harness itself — the perceive-reason-act-feedback loop, the tool registry, the context manager, the control layer — and shows how a language model becomes a worker. **Chapter 16 — Agent Economics** asks what happens when this worker becomes cheap and abundant: how production functions are rewritten, which jobs grow and which collapse, what new bottlenecks appear in the place of the old ones.

And then, at the very end of the book, comes a final chapter that is not a prediction but a backward glance — a chance to look down the long line of harnesses we have been following, from the leather strap on the ox to the software framework around the model, and notice what they all turn out to have in common. The reins, as the farmer once knew them, are still in our hands. The question is what they are now attached to, and how firmly we choose to hold on.
